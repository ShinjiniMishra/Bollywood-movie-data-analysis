//PROJECT OVERVIEW//
This exploratory data analysis project studies the various components in the bollywood movie industry through correlation, visualization, and thorough analysis. 
From the analysis, I have tried to bring out certain important insights and relationships between the most crucial elements in the industry—such as budget, ratings, 
and profit—and visualize them through statistical diagrams.

//TOOLS USED//
Python
Pandas
Matplotlib
Seaborn

//KEY FINDINGS//
1.Genre

- Genre that dominated mostly throughout 2017 to 2025 was
  War/Biographical and thus had higher average ratings in the dataset

- Genre with the least average rating was Action/Fantasy in the dataset


2. Year

- 2021 was the year with the highest rated movies in the dataset

- 2019 and 2023 had movies with the lowest ratings

- 2017, 2024 and 2025 had the maximum number of movie releases
  in the dataset

- 2020 and 2021 had the least number of movie releases mainly
  due to the Covid pandemic


3. Correlation Heatmap Findings

- Budget of a movie and its rating have a negative correlation,
  meaning the rating of a movie is not influenced by its budget.
  A big budget movie does not ensure high ratings

- Actor count and budget have a strong positive correlation,
  meaning more the actor count, higher will be the budget
  of that particular movie

- Revenue and profit also have a strong positive correlation,
  meaning higher the revenue, higher will be the profit
  obtained from that movie

- Budget and profit of a movie have no correlation with the
  year in which it is released. This is expected as production
  costs and market sizes differ significantly across years

- Movies with higher or lower profit do not necessarily have
  high or low ratings. Rating of a movie has no correlation
  with its profit

- Actor count and revenue of a movie have no correlation,
  signifying that a movie with more or less actors does not
  guarantee high or low revenue
